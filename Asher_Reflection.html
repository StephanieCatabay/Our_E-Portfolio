ACTIVITY 1

1. Among the principles of learning, which one is the most important for you? Substantiate your answer.

For me, the most important principle of learning is that learning is an active process. As what I have observed in grade 6 students they learn best when they are directly involved in activities that allow them to explore, ask questions, and apply what they learn in real-life situations. Active learning encourages deeper understanding and helps learners retain knowledge longer because they experience and construct meaning on their own.

2. What insight did you learn from the class activities that you have observed?

From the class activities I observed, I learned that effective teaching requires creativity and flexibility. A teacher must use different strategies to address the learners’ needs and interests. I also realized that students are more engaged when the lessons are connected to their daily lives, making learning more meaningful and enjoyable.

Through this activity, I realized that teaching is not just about delivering lessons but about creating meaningful experiences that help learners connect knowledge with real-life situations. A good teacher knows how to apply various learning principles and strategies to make students active participants in their learning journey.

I would make the environment more conducive to learning by maintaining cleanliness, ensuring safety, and fostering respect among learners. Classroom management has a great impact on learning because it sets the tone for discipline, focus, and collaboration. When rules and routines are clear, students know what is expected of them, which minimizes distractions and allows more time for meaningful learning. A well-managed classroom also builds a sense of responsibility and respect among students.

My ideal learning environment would be bright, organized, and filled with learning materials. Desks would be arranged to encourage group activities and discussions. There would be colorful bulletin boards displaying student works, and a small reading corner to promote a love for learning. The atmosphere would be positive and inclusive, where every student feels safe, valued, and inspired.

Through this activity, I realized that a well-managed and positive learning environment greatly influences students’ behavior, motivation, and performance. A teacher plays a vital role in shaping this environment by maintaining order, showing empathy, and encouraging participation. Creating an ideal classroom means balancing structure with creativity to ensure that every learner can thrive academically and emotionally.

ACTIVITY 5

1. As a millennial would-be teacher, how can the assessment process help you make learning more fun and more meaningful?

By using creative activities such as ratio games, visual aids, and real-life applications like cooking or budgeting, I can make the assessment process both fun and meaningful for students.

2. As a future teacher, how can you eliminate fear of assessment to your students?

I would encourage students to see assessments as learning opportunities, not punishments. Giving positive feedback, allowing retakes, and celebrating small improvements can lessen their anxiety.

3. If you are the teacher, what solution(s) could you offer to address the challenges in evaluating students’ performance and giving feedbacks?

I would use organized record sheets or digital grading tools, prepare clear rubrics, and provide quick feedback after each quiz or activity.

4. If you are the teacher, how do you formulate a plan for your assessment tools in an online platform teaching?

I would use online quizzes through Google Forms, digital worksheets for solving ratios, and virtual group tasks like creating a slideshow on ratio applications. I would also provide immediate feedback through comments or chat.

Through this activity, I learned that assessment plays a vital role in improving both teaching and learning. In teaching ratio, the use of quizzes, performance tasks, and real-life applications helped make lessons more meaningful. I realized that assessment is not just about giving grades but also about guiding learners to understand their progress and build confidence.
